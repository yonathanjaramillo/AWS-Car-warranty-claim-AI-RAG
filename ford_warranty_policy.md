# Ford Motor Company — Warranty Policy Guidelines 2024
## Section 1: Coverage Overview
Ford Motor Company provides warranty coverage for all new vehicles sold by authorized Ford dealers. The warranty covers defects in materials or workmanship under normal use and service.

## Section 2: Basic Coverage (Bumper-to-Bumper)
- Duration: 3 years or 36,000 miles, whichever occurs first
- Coverage: All components except normal wear items
- Transferable to subsequent owners within coverage period

## Section 3: Powertrain Coverage
- Duration: 5 years or 60,000 miles, whichever occurs first
- Covered components: Engine, transmission, drivetrain
- Labor op codes covered: 06-10A, 06-10B, 07-10A, 07-20B, 08-01A

## Section 4: Labor Operation Codes and Coverage
### Section 4.1: Engine Service
- Labor Op 06-10A: Engine oil change — covered under basic warranty
- Labor Op 06-10B: Engine oil and filter change — covered under basic warranty
- Labor Op 06-20A: Engine tune-up — covered if failure due to defect
- Maximum approved labor rate: $125/hour

### Section 4.2: Transmission Service
- Labor Op 07-10A: Transmission fluid change — covered under powertrain
- Labor Op 07-20B: Transmission repair — covered under powertrain warranty
- Parts must be genuine Ford OEM parts

### Section 4.3: Brake Service
- Labor Op 09-10A: Brake pad replacement — NOT covered (normal wear)
- Labor Op 09-20B: Brake rotor replacement — covered if defect confirmed

## Section 5: Claim Amount Guidelines
- Maximum claim amount per repair order: $5,000
- Claims exceeding $2,500 require pre-authorization
- Labor amounts must not exceed approved labor time guide
- Parts must be invoiced at dealer cost plus 30% markup maximum

## Section 6: VIN Verification Requirements
- VIN must be 17 characters, alphanumeric
- Vehicle must be within warranty coverage window at repair date
- Mileage must be verified against odometer reading at time of service

## Section 7: Dealer Requirements
- Dealer must be Ford authorized at time of repair
- Technician must hold current Ford certification
- Repair order must be completed within 30 days of repair date

## Section 8: Exclusions
- Damage from accidents, misuse, or neglect
- Normal wear items: tires, brake pads, wiper blades, filters (unless defective)
- Modifications or non-Ford parts
- Damage from improper maintenance
